===============================================================================
FUNCTIONALITY OVERVIEW
===============================================================================

INPUT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Requires: Monthly district-level DataFrame with columns:
  - month (datetime)
  - state (string)
  - district (string)
  - demographic_updates (int)
  - biometric_updates (int)
  - enrolments (int)
  - anomaly_flag (boolean)
  - total_updates (derived: demographic + biometric)
  - update_rate (derived: total_updates / enrolments)

PROCESSING
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Three-step algorithm:

Step 1: CAPACITY INCREASE DETECTION
  • Identify sustained positive growth periods
  • Require ≥2 consecutive months of growth
  • Use statistical thresholding to filter noise
  
Step 2: ANOMALY RATE COMPARISON
  • Measure pre-capacity anomaly frequency
  • Measure post-capacity anomaly frequency
  • Calculate percentage change
  
Step 3: ELASTICITY SCORING & CLASSIFICATION
  • Score: 0 (highly responsive) to 1 (unresponsive)
  • Classify: High / Moderate / Low elasticity
  • Recommend: Actionable policy interventions

OUTPUT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Three mandatory outputs:

1. DISTRICT-LEVEL SUMMARY (one row per district)
   Columns:
   • state
   • district
   • elasticity_score (0.0 → 1.0)
   • elasticity_classification (High / Moderate / Low)
   • expected_intervention_effectiveness
   • policy_recommendation (actionable)

2. STATE-LEVEL SUMMARY
   Columns:
   • state
   • total_districts_analyzed
   • high/moderate/low_elasticity_districts
   • capacity_responsive_pct
   • low_elasticity_pct
   • avg_elasticity_score

3. INSIGHT GENERATOR FUNCTION
   generate_insight(state, district) → plain-language explanation
   
   Example: "Historical analysis of Bangalore district shows strong capacity 
   elasticity: periods of increased operational capacity have consistently been 
   followed by reduced anomaly frequency. This demonstrates clear operational 
   responsiveness. Recommendation: Continue expansion strategy"

===============================================================================
ELASTICITY CLASSIFICATION GUIDE
===============================================================================

HIGH ELASTICITY (score < 0.33)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✓ Meaning: Capacity increases clearly reduce anomalies
✓ Implication: System is responsive to improvements
✓ Action: Continue and expand capacity investments
✓ Example: Doubling capacity cuts anomalies by 40%

MODERATE ELASTICITY (0.33 ≤ score < 0.67)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
⚠ Meaning: Capacity helps, but anomalies persist
⚠ Implication: Both capacity AND structural issues exist
⚠ Action: Investigate root causes while expanding capacity
⚠ Common Causes: Data quality, process design, external factors

LOW ELASTICITY (score ≥ 0.67)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✗ Meaning: Capacity increases don't reduce anomalies
✗ Implication: Problem is structural, not capacity-driven
✗ Action: Investigate and address root causes
✗ Common Causes: Equipment issues, data quality, process flaws

===============================================================================
KEY METRICS & INTERPRETATION
===============================================================================

elasticity_score (0.0 to 1.0)
  0.0 ..................... 1.0
  Highly Responsive      Unresponsive
  (Capacity Works)    (Capacity Doesn't Help)

capacity_responsive_pct (state-level)
  % of districts where capacity increases are effective
  > 70%: Strong capacity strategy works
  40-70%: Mixed results, investigate
  < 40%: Structural focus needed

low_elasticity_pct (state-level)
  % of districts with persistent anomalies despite capacity
  > 40%: Critical - systemic issues prevalent
  20-40%: Moderate - investigate select districts
  < 20%: Good - mostly responsive districts
